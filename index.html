Thesis
Villa Mater

Marco Vargas is a Portuguese architect. He studied at FAUP, where he developed his master’s thesis, Villa Mater: a house in the Azores, under the supervision of Patrícia da Silva. The project explores an underground house organized around a single patio, where space becomes a retreat from the world.

During his studies, he participated in two editions of Porto Academy. In 2016, he won first prize in the GO! Architecture competition for AEFAUP and first prize in the Warsaw Under Construction Festival 8 during his curricular internship at BUDCUD in Kraków.

He began his professional career at EBEJER BONNICI in Malta, contributing to projects including the renovation of an apartment in a historic building in London for the artist Anna Pesonen, the restoration and renovation of an 18th-century palazzo in Birkirkara, and the design of three single-family residences in Naxxar.

Currently, he collaborates on spaces idealised by the artist Ellen Nielsen.

For Marco Vargas, architecture is not a response to a brief, but the creation of conditions for life to unfold freely. It does not define us, but allows us to define ourselves. A space to let us be.
