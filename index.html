Marco Vargas is an architect based in the Azores, Portugal, working in contemporary architecture with a focus on dwelling, materiality, and place. His thesis project, Villa Mater: a House in Azores, investigates the relationship between architecture and landscape in the Atlantic archipelago.

His architectural thinking is informed by the work of Álvaro Siza Vieira, Peter Zumthor, Valerio Olgiati, Kazuo Shinohara, SANAA, Ebejer Bonnici, Patricia da Silva, Ryue Nishizawa, Junya Ishigami, Go Hasegawa and Tomoaki Uno — architects whose practices engage with silence, structure, and the poetics of space.
