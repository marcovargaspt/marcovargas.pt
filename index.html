Thesis
Villa Mater

Marco Vargas was born in 1991 in Portugal. His formation as an architect began at FAUP, where his master's thesis, "Villa Mater: a house in the Azores", was supervised by the Architect Patrícia da Silva. An underground house conceived around a single patio, where the experience of space becomes a retreat from the world.

During his academic years, he participated in two editions of Porto Academy under the guidance of architects Raphael Zuber and Kersten Geers. In 2016, he won first prize at the GO! Architecture for AEFAUP and first prize at the Warsaw Under Construction Festival 8, during his curricular internship at BUDCUD in Kraków.

His professional career began at EBEJER BONNICI in Malta, where among his works was the renovation of an apartment in a historic building in London for the artist Anna Pesonen, the restoration and renovation of an 18th-century palazzo in Birkirkara, and the design of three single-family residences in Naxxar. It was there that he learned to reconcile the rigour of a position with the demands of the real world. That ideas only matter when they survive contact with reality.

Currently he collaborates on spaces idealised by the artist Ellen Nielsen.

Marco Vargas does not believe that architecture is a service to answer a brief, nor a formal experiment performed for an audience. It is, at its core, the creation of conditions for human life to unfold freely. A space that does not define us, but allows us to define ourselves. A space to let us be.
